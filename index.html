Healthy Life

Most heart and diabetic patients are treated by specialist doctors. They do not tempt in getting treatment from any doctor other than that specific doctor. Patients have to meet the doctor once in a certain period of time. With the busy schedule of doctors, patients should wait for a week to meet them. Most times, during this waiting period, the medicines need to control the condition of the patient may be over. This often prompts patients to take risky actions such as retaking previous medications. Because of this, the diabetics and heart risk conditions of the patients can become more serious. We are introducing this system in an effort to avoid this critical situation. As mentioned before guides on personal guidance systems for both heart and diabetes patients are rare. So, there should be a better solution for the personal guidance system for heart and diabetes patients. Both heart and cholesterol diseases can be managed by managing heart-healthy diets containing meal plans, managing stress, and day-to-day exercises.

2. Research Gap

2.1 Risk Prediction
The purpose of identifying diabetes and heart risk with high accuracy is still a very challenging issue. There are a variety of characteristics that contribute to the prediction of diabetes and heart risk, but it is especially difficult to identify those features and make use of those characteristics to diagnose diabetes at an early stage. Thus, analyzing the qualities and attributes for prediction purposes is a complex process. Research has been happening during the previous many decades, and the problem is still accessible for research. After evaluating the study done by various contributors and researchers, it has been concluded that we cannot foresee which elements from the dataset have a significant function, and optimized feature selection cannot provide 100% accuracy. Machine learning classification techniques deployed by 99% of the researchers comprise naïve Bayes, support vector machine, decision trees, random forest, logistic regression, multilayer perceptron, and k-nearest neighbor. most of the products use manual input for attributes of those machine learning. But we cannot trust the user input. So, we are proposing a simple IoT device for collecting user health details. The device can measure users' common body health details (Heart Rate, Body Temperature) and send them to the mobile.
The above-mentioned literature survey clearly demonstrates the necessity for an early detection system that can identify both heart and diabetes because so little research has been focused on it based on trust user data. Today all mobile devices are equipped with health maintenance applications. As an example, Samsung has a 'Samsung Health' built-in mobile app. But those applications cannot predict Heart and Diabetics risks and cannot guide to control those. Via the proposed system, Users can Identify Heart and Diabetics risks by using gathered user data via the external device. In using this approach, the system can gather more trust data and users want to enter less number of data into the system. In the currently available mobile system, there is no adequate mechanism for predicting and controlling heart and diabetes. We have identified some similarities and differences. 3.2 Meal Planner
Cardiovascular disease is the leading cause of death worldwide, accounting for 16% of all fatalities. Because of this, passing has increased significantly since 2000, rising by more than 2 million to 8.9 million passing in 2019. In 2014, 8.5% of persons who were 18 or older had diabetes. A total of 1.5 million deaths were directly related to diabetes in 2019, and 48% of these deaths occurred in people under the age of 70. Diabetes patients frequently develop heart problems. According to National Cardiac Association data from 2012, 65% of diabetics will pass away from a heart condition or a stroke. People with diabetes are more than twice as likely to die from heart disease and stroke. A person's risk for heart disease and diabetes can also be increased by a number of other medical issues and lifestyle choices, including sedentary lifestyles, fast-food habits, obesity, overweight, and bad diets. In impoverished nations like Sri Lanka, where there is a dearth of medical diagnostic equipment and specialists, heart disease diagnosis and treatment are particularly difficult. So, for a diabetic and a heart patient, a realistic and accurate risk prediction of diabetic diseases, heart-related diseases, and guidance for maintaining a healthy life are important. According to the survey, many individuals are aware of heart disease and diabetes, but the majority of them are unaware that the number of people dying from both of these conditions is rising daily. Therefore, changes in the world's food system, dietary habits, and potential governmental responses have a negative impact on leading a healthy life. Therefore, the food system would have more potent motivating incentives to deliver greater advantages for them if more individuals made better choices. People are also aware that eating poorly, consuming unhealthy fats, and consuming processed meats can increase their susceptibility to a number of diseases. The survey's findings indicate that people are aware that improper eating habits can lead to both heart disease and diabetes, and that a good diet can reduce the risk of both conditions. As a result, a lot of them employ personal trainers or schedule t heir workouts in gyms. Even so, since they might not be able to pay personal trainers or fitness sessions but still want to adhere to good diet plans, some people need to keep track of their meals. However, the issue is that, despite people's best efforts to eat healthily, there is no such personal guidance for leading a healthy life that provides daily recommendations for healthy meals. As a result, many people believe that managing their hectic lifestyles will be made easier by following the advice provided by an app like this. Therefore, this study recommends "Healthy Diet Planner" as a companion that aids individuals in managing their living circumstances by leading a healthy lifestyle.
